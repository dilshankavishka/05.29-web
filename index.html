Violet Evergarden


Violet Evergarden is a captivating Japanese light novel series written by Kana Akatsuki and beautifully illustrated by Akiko Takase. Published by Kyoto Animation under their KA Esuma Bunko imprint, the series ran from December 2015 to March 2020. The heartwarming story revolves around Violet Evergarden, a young ex-soldier who finds herself employed at the CH Postal Company. Her unique role as an Auto Memory Doll involves writing letters for people who cannot express their emotions or need assistance in communicating. Violet’s journey of reintegration into society after the war, coupled with her search for purpose, unfolds as she grapples with the last words spoken by her mentor and guardian, Major Gilbert: “I love you.” The anime adaptation, consisting of a 13-episode television series, an original video animation episode, and two films (Violet Evergarden: Eternity and the Auto Memory Doll and Violet Evergarden: The Movie), beautifully captures the essence of Violet’s emotional exploration and growth. Intriguingly, the term “Auto Memory Doll” originated from a prototype typewriter invented by Professor Orlando, designed to register spoken words. Violet’s journey is one of self-discovery, healing, and the power of heartfelt communication.
